================================================================================
PPE DETECTION PROJECT: LIMITATIONS & DISCUSSION REFERENCE
================================================================================

This document summarizes the root causes behind the discrepancy between the high 
validation accuracy (~95%) and the lower real-world generalization accuracy (~60%), 
along with concrete, actionable solutions. Use these points to write Section 12 
(Discussion) and Section 13 (Limitations) of the project report.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. THE DISCREPANCY: WHY DOES IT HAPPEN?
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

A. SHORTCUT LEARNING & BACKGROUND BIAS
   - Cause: The frozen pre-trained ResNet18 model is highly sensitive to global 
     visual contexts like color, textures, lighting, and backgrounds. If the 
     "Hardhat" or "Safety Vest" training crops consistently contain specific 
     outdoor background elements, and "NO-" classes contain indoor elements, the 
     Random Forest learns to classify the background rather than the PPE item.
   - Real-World Effect: When tested on new backgrounds, the model loses its 
     shortcut context, causing predictions to fail.

B. MEDIAPIPE LANDMARK DRIFTS & CROPPING NOISE
   - Cause: In real-world environments, motion blur, unusual angles, or physical 
     occlusions cause MediaPipe to estimate landmarks with offsets. 
   - Real-World Effect: Instead of a clean, centered crop of the PPE, the model 
     receives a cropped box containing 80% background noise and only 20% of the 
     object. The binary Random Forest classifier is forced to make a decision 
     on what is essentially background.

C. THE OUTLIER FILTERING PARADOX
   - Cause: The training process uses IQR outlier filtering to clean up the 
     dataset (resulting in 'resnet_embeddings_filtered.csv'). 
   - Real-World Effect: While outlier removal significantly boosts validation 
     accuracy on the clean training set, it deprives the Random Forest of 
     learning how to handle noisy, blurry, or edge-case images. The real world 
     is full of these "outliers".

D. DETECTIONS ON NON-VISIBLE/OCCLUDED PARTS
   - Cause: When a hand or torso is not visible, MediaPipe still attempts to 
     estimate its coordinates, generating a crop of background pixels (e.g., 
     walls, floor, sky).
   - Real-World Effect: The background crop is processed by the Gloves classifier, 
     leading to arbitrary predictions and ghost detections (e.g., predicting 
     "Gloves" or "NO-Gloves" on an empty wall).

E. THE GLOVE CLASSIFIER PARADOX (RESOLUTION & COLOR OVERFITTING)
   - Cause: Gloves lack a rigid geometric shape (unlike helmets/vests) and are 
     extremely small physically. When hand crops are resized and upscaled to 224x224, 
     they suffer from severe pixelation, compression artifacts, and high background 
     contamination (jeans, sleeves, tools). The ResNet18 model therefore relies 
     on color and texture shortcuts (e.g., bare skin tone vs. blue/white glove color).
   - Real-World Effect: Despite having the highest validation accuracy (~97.3%), 
     the model collapses in the real world when encountering different skin tones, 
     alternative glove colors, varying camera resolutions, or new backgrounds.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
2. PROPOSED SOLUTIONS & FUTURE RECOMMENDATIONS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

A. MEDIAPIPE CONFIDENCE THRESHOLDING
   - Solution: Leverage MediaPipe's internal `visibility` and `presence` scores. 
     If the visibility score of a landmark falls below a set threshold (e.g., 0.6), 
     reject the crop immediately and output 'NOT_VISIBLE' instead of classifying 
     background noise.

B. END-TO-END FINE-TUNING OF THE FEATURE EXTRACTOR
   - Solution: Instead of keeping the ResNet18 weights frozen, fine-tune the 
     entire ResNet18 model on the target PPE crop dataset. This forces the model 
     to discard background context and learn semantic features directly related to 
     the physical properties of hardhats, vests, and gloves.

C. ROBUST DATA AUGMENTATION
   - Solution: During training, apply heavy data augmentations directly to the 
     cropped objects. This includes:
     - Random bounding box padding and shifts (to simulate MediaPipe drift).
     - Color jittering and lighting changes.
     - Background replacement (swapping backgrounds randomly to break context bias).

D. SPECIALIZED HAND ESTIMATORS & COLOR JITTERING
   - Solution: 
     - Use a dedicated hand-pose estimator (such as MediaPipe Hands) specifically 
       for hand crops to extract higher resolution, tighter bounding boxes.
     - Apply aggressive color jittering and hue/saturation shifts during training 
       to prevent the classifier from overfitting to specific skin tones or 
       particular glove colors.
